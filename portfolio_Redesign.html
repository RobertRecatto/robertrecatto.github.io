Music App Redesign



For this project, myself and a group of 3 others teamed up to rethink the design of mobile music apps.

We designed our new mockups using a vast amount of research from user interviews to even user testing.

We focused on the task of simplifying created and managing a playlist, and asked questions and ran tests specifically catering to this task.

In the end we used all of our data to compile a new design that shortened steps it takes to manipulate playlists compared to other leading competitors.

What We Found


We interviewed users in order to learn when they use playlists, why they use playlists, if they even thought about it, we wanted to know.

We used these interviews to lay out some use cases for playlists, and traced out the steps to accomplish these use cases on other apps in the market.

For the most common use cases we even watched users using our competitors applications in order to discover where they had difficulties using those applications.

Using this data we discovered which tasks could be simplified and how to simplify them.

What We Decided


Our first difficult design decision was what to display on the homepage. We decided to offer users their favorite genre's. This helps users easily find songs they want to start off their playlists.

We also struggled with naming conventions for our navbar. Again, here we focused on the task of helping users with playlist management and opted into giving playlists their own tab for easy access.

We noticed while observing our users that many were struggling with finding the options menu for songs so we put the option menu in the same place for both songs and playlists to increase ease of use and consistency.

A lot of the issues we notices our users run into during testing were also due to a clutter of features on the screen. To avoid this we tried to make a clear sequence of events for each function, and cut out functionality that wasn't needed.

What I learned


In the end, this project helped me realize that I previously did not truly understand the design process.

It helped me stray away from my previous inclination to haphazardly throw things on the page to adopt a more calculated design approach.

The project showed me that there should be a reason why a design is the way it is and that reason should be backed with hard data and testing.
